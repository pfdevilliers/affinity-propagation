What is it?

Affinity Propagation form clusters of data points around a exemplar by considering all the data points as potential exemplars simultaneously. Messages are passed between data points until suitable clusters and exemplars are found.

How does it work?

  1. Each data point i sends a responsibility r(i,k) to a candidate exemplar indicating how much it favours the candidate exemplar over other candidates.
  2. Availabilities a(i,k) are sent from candidate exemplars to data points indicating how well the candidate exemplar can act as a cluster center for the data points.
  3. After each iteration availability and responsibility can be combined to identify the exemplars, a(i,k) + r(i,k).

Further Reading

Dueck, D. and Frey, B.J.,Clustering by Passing Messages between Data points, University of Toronto Science 315, p. 972-976, February 2007
